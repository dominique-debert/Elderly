Nutritional Advice for Seniors

Protein

Protein is essential for maintaining muscle mass and strength, which is particularly important as we age. Aim for lean sources like poultry, fish, beans, and tofu. Incorporate protein into each meal to support overall health and vitality.

Fiber

Fiber aids digestion and helps prevent constipation, a common issue among seniors. Good sources include fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes. Gradually increase your fiber intake to avoid discomfort.

Vitamins

Seniors may need more of certain vitamins, such as D and B12. Vitamin D supports bone health, while B12 is crucial for nerve function. Consult your doctor about supplements if you're not getting enough from your diet.

Seasonal Recommendations

Adjust your diet with the seasons. In summer, focus on hydrating fruits and vegetables like watermelon and cucumbers. In winter, opt for hearty soups and stews with root vegetables and lean meats to stay warm and nourished.
